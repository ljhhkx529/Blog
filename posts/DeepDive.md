### **Technical Breakdown: Why the D200 Disrupts the Market**

As the Captain noted, the D200 isn't just a "smaller" engine; it’s a **higher-torque** solution. Here is why his 200HP Diesel is outperforming his expectations of what 200HP should feel like:

#### **1. The Torque Advantage (400 N·m)**
Gasoline engines rely on high RPMs to find power. Diesel engines, like the D200, deliver massive torque at lower RPMs. This is why the Captain felt "no difference" between an empty boat and one with 7 passengers. The **400 N·m of torque** ensures that the prop stays hooked up and pushing, regardless of the load.

#### **2. The 50% Fuel Efficiency Gap**
* **300HP Gasoline:** ~90 L/h
* **200HP Parsun Diesel:** ~42 L/h
* **The Math:** For a commercial operator running 500 hours a year, this switch saves tens of thousands of dollars in fuel alone, effectively paying for the engine in the first 18 months.

#### **3. Operational Longevity**
Diesel engines are built for heavy-duty cycles. While a gasoline engine "screams" at high RPMs to maintain speed, the D200 hums at a stable 3800 RPM, leading to less internal wear and significantly longer service intervals.
