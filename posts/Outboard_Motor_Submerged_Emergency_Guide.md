# So can an outboard motor be submerged in water?

### 1. Core Structure: Three-Section

Outboard motors, as a very popular marine device in modern times, have undergone many generations of design changes, but their fundamental structure remains primarily a three-section structure. If some parts are submerged in water, the consequences can be very serious.

* **Powerhead:** The most crucial part at the top of the machine (must not be submerged), containing cylinders, pistons, and the electronic control system.

* **Midsection:** Provides structural support, containing the mounting brackets, steering mechanism, and exhaust cooling channels.

* **Lower Unit:** Contains the gearbox and propeller; must be submerged in water to operate.

* **Cylinder Failure (Hydrolock):** Water entering the cylinder can cause connecting rods to bend or the cylinder head to crack, resulting in irreversible physical damage.

* **Circuit and Corrosion:** Modern electronic components are highly susceptible to short circuits, and residual moisture (especially seawater) can cause metals to rust rapidly.

### 4. First Aid Guide: The Golden Rules for Rescuing Drowning Machines

In case of an accident, the key to rescue is speed and thoroughness, and pulling the rope to start the machine is strictly prohibited.

* **Cleaning and Draining:** Thoroughly rinse with fresh water and remove the spark plugs to drain water from the cylinder and fuel system.

* **Lubrication and Repair:** Inject rust-preventive oil into the cylinder and immediately have it handled by a professional technician to prevent bearing failure due to even a small amount of residual water.
